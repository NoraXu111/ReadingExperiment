Consent of Participant

 

Title: Testing Transsaccadic Working Memory Online

 

The purpose of this study is to identify some of the factors that influence how well our understanding when we read texts.

To this end I will perform a reading comprehension task where I read short passages of text (presented as a paragraph or as one word at a time) and then answer multiple-choice questions. I will also be asked to complete a self-report questionnaires. There are no know risks to the study other than those of daily life.

Data on my performance will be used exclusively for scientific purposes, which might include publication in a scientific journal. However, my data will be recorded and maintained in confidence by, and available only to Dr. Niemeier and researchers working under his supervision on password-protected computers. Further, the research ethics program may have confidential access to data to help ensure participant protection procedures are followed. That is, the research study that I am participating in may be reviewed for quality assurance to make sure that the required laws and guidelines are followed. If chosen, (a) representative(s) of the Human Research Ethics Program (HREP) may access study-related data and/or consent materials as part of the review. All information accessed by the HREP will be upheld to the same level of confidentiality that has been stated by the research team.

My identity will not be identifiable from any presentation or publication of the results of this study. Should I be interested in a summary of the research results, I can contact Dr. Niemeier by email or phone at a later time.

For compensation, I will receive 0.5 credits or $5 for every half hour I spend (rounded up). This experiment will take approximately 1.5 hour to complete (for a total of 1.5 credit or $15).

During the experiment I will be allowed to take breaks whenever instructed to do so.

I am aware that I do not have to disclose any information that I do not feel comfortable disclosing. I am also aware that I may withdraw from the experiment at any time during its duration without penalty. If I chose to do so, my data will be removed/destroyed entirely, and I will be compensated for the time I spent on the study before withdrawal.

I have read and understand the information presented above about the procedures and risks involved in this study and have received satisfactory answers to my questions related to this study. I understand that if I have any questions or concerns resulting from my participation in this study, I may contact the experimenter or the laboratory director (Dr. Matthias Niemeier, 419-287-7466, m.niemeier@utoronto.ca, UTSC, room SW 408), or the Office of Research Ethics at ethics.review@utoronto.ca or 416-946-3273, if I have questions about my rights as participants. With full knowledge of all foregoing in this document I agree, of my own free will, to participate in this study.
